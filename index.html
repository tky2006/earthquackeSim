🌍 How to Play
Overview — 1/4
Welcome, structural engineer! Your job is to design buildings that can survive earthquakes across 10 increasingly dangerous rounds.

You start with 3 lives ❤️. Lose a life if your structure collapses or the timer runs out. Survive all 10 rounds to win!
🧱 Choose Materials
Building basics — 2/4
Each material has different Strength, Flexibility, and Weight.

Wood — light & flexible, but low strength.
Brick — strong but brittle. Cracks under heavy shaking.
Steel — excellent strength AND flexibility.
Stone — very strong, zero flex. Good for moderate quakes.
Composite — balanced across all stats. A safe bet.
🔺 Choose Structure
Shape matters — 3/4
Your building's shape affects how stable it is under seismic force.

Box — simple, low stability.
Cross — reinforced X-bracing. Medium stability.
Triangle — triangles distribute force. High stability.
Dome — arch geometry. Maximum stability under compression.

Survival formula: Str + Stability + (Flex × 0.5) ≥ Magnitude × 1.5
⚡ Scoring & Difficulty
The challenge — 4/4
Rounds 1–4 (Easy): Magnitude 3.0–4.5, 30 seconds
Rounds 5–7 (Medium): Magnitude 5.0–6.5, 25 seconds
Rounds 8–10 (Hard): Magnitude 7.0–9.0, 20 seconds

Score = (Survived × 20) + (Perfect design × 30) + (Speed bonus)

Stars are earned based on your overall performance across all rounds.
